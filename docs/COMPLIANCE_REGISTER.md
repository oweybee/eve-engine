## Purpose

A single, auditable record of MaxEdge's regulatory obligations, who owns each
one, how we comply, and what evidence exists. Keep it current — the position
must be defensible as MaxEdge grows.

## Business classification

MaxEdge is an **information and analytics service**, not a gambling operator.
It does **not** accept bets, hold or pool client funds, operate a book, or
guarantee outcomes — so it does not require a UK Gambling Commission operating
licence for the core product. This classification is deliberate and must be
protected. **The regulatory exposure comes from marketing, not the tool.**

Red lines that would change our category (do **not** cross without licensing
and legal sign-off):

- Accepting stakes or pooling user money.
- Placing or brokering bets on a user's behalf.
- Guaranteeing profit or outcomes.

## The September 2025 CAP Code change (critical) {#cap-2025}

On **1 September 2025** the ASA extended the CAP Code so that **non-paid,
organic marketing now falls within scope**. Implications for MaxEdge:

- **Organic content counts.** Telegram posts, social content, blogs and
  influencer content promoting gambling must comply — paid or not.
- **Non-UK loophole closed.** Rules apply regardless of whether the promoted
  operator is UK-licensed or offshore.
- **Section 16 covers third parties.** Affiliates acting on an advertiser's
  behalf are explicitly in scope.
- **Consultation ran to 1 Dec 2025** on proportionality and whether affiliates
  should be treated differently from operators — detail may still shift.

## Compliance posture

1. **Subscription-first.** Paid subscriptions are the revenue core; this
   minimises the gambling-advertising surface vs an affiliate model.
2. **Own the "information service" framing.** Consistently position MaxEdge as
   analytics/education — model output and market data — not a tipster or a
   route to a bookmaker.
3. **Compliance review before each new channel.** Vet Telegram, social and any
   creator partnerships against the CAP Code before scaling.
4. **Specialist legal sign-off.** Engage UK gambling-advertising counsel before
   launching paid tiers or any affiliate line; re-check post-consultation.
5. **Document everything.** Keep this register current so the position is
   auditable.

## Pre-launch checklist (before charging for a paid tier)

- [ ] Legal review of `/terms` and `/privacy` by qualified counsel.
- [ ] Data-protection review; register with the ICO if required; appoint a DP contact.
- [ ] Confirm payment processor terms (Stripe) and PCI posture.
- [ ] Gambling-advertising counsel sign-off on marketing & any affiliate line.
- [ ] Confirm ≥3 months of settled, CLV-backed public track record exists.
- [ ] Significant terms drafted for any referral/incentive scheme.
